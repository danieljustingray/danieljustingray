Genesis

  • Creation: God creates the heavens and the earth, and mankind in His image.
  • The Fall: Adam and Eve sin, bringing death and separation from God.
  • God's Promise: God's covenant with Abraham to make him the father of many nations.
  • The Patriarchs: The stories of the patriarchs, including Abraham, Isaac, Jacob (Israel), and Joseph.
  • God’s Purpose: God’s plan for the blessing of all nations through Abraham's descendants.

Exodus

  • The Exodus: God delivers Israel from Egyptian bondage, leading them through the Red Sea.
  • The Covenant: God establishes His covenant with Israel at Mount Sinai, giving the law.
  • The Tabernacle: Detailed instructions for the building of the Tabernacle, God's dwelling place.
  • God’s Presence: The significance of God's presence with His people in the wilderness.

Leviticus

  • Holiness: The theme of holiness and God's commands for the priesthood and sacrifices.
  • The Sacrificial System: Detailed laws about sacrifices, offerings, and rituals for atonement.
  • The Day of Atonement: The central day for the forgiveness of sins.
  • Laws of Purity: Instructions regarding clean and unclean animals, bodily discharges, and more.
  • Feasts and Festivals: Observances such as Passover, the Feast of Tabernacles, and others.

Numbers

  • The Census: A census of the tribes of Israel and their arrangement around the Tabernacle.
  • The Wilderness Journey: The journey through the wilderness, God’s provision, and the Israelites' disobedience.
  • Rebellion: Several instances of rebellion against God, including the golden calf incident and Korah’s rebellion.
  • The Promised Land: Preparation for entering the promised land and the leadership transition to Joshua.

Deuteronomy

  • Moses' Farewell: Moses delivers a series of speeches to the Israelites before his death.
  • Reaffirmation of the Covenant: Moses reaffirms God’s covenant with Israel, emphasizing obedience and blessings.
  • The Shema: The central declaration of Jewish faith: "Hear, O Israel, the Lord our God is one."
  • Preparation for the Promised Land: Instructions for life in the land of Canaan, including leadership, justice, and worship.

Joshua

  • Conquest of Canaan: Israel’s military campaigns to conquer the promised land under Joshua’s leadership.
  • Dividing the Land: The allocation of the land among the twelve tribes of Israel.
  • Covenant Renewal: Joshua renews the covenant with Israel and urges faithfulness to God.

Judges

  • Cycles of Apostasy: The Israelites fall into cycles of sin, oppression, repentance, and deliverance.
  • The Judges: God raises up leaders (judges) like Deborah, Gideon, and Samson to deliver Israel from its enemies.
  • Decline: Moral and spiritual decline as Israel does what is right in its own eyes.

Ruth

  • Story of Ruth: Ruth, a Moabite widow, remains faithful to her mother-in-law Naomi and finds redemption through Boaz.
  • God’s Providence: The story illustrates God’s care and providence in the lives of individuals.
  • Line of David: Ruth is an ancestor of King David, showing God’s plan for the Messiah.

1 Samuel

  • Samuel’s Birth: The birth and calling of Samuel as a prophet of God.
  • Saul’s Kingship: Saul is anointed as Israel's first king but later disobeys God.
  • David’s Rise: The anointing of David as king, his battle with Goliath, and his rise to prominence.
  • Saul’s Decline: Saul’s jealousy and decline, leading to David’s eventual kingship.

2 Samuel

  • David’s Reign: David becomes king and unites Israel, establishing Jerusalem as the capital.
  • David’s Sin: David’s sin with Bathsheba and its consequences on his family and kingdom.
  • David’s Heart for God: Despite his failures, David’s heart remains loyal to God, and God makes a covenant with him.

1 Kings

  • Solomon’s Reign: Solomon succeeds David, builds the Temple, and reigns in peace and wisdom.
  • Division of the Kingdom: After Solomon’s death, the kingdom divides into Israel (north) and Judah (south).

2 Kings

  • Decline and Fall of Israel and Judah: The kingdom continues to decline due to disobedience and idolatry.
  • Prophets: The ministries of prophets like Elijah and Elisha, calling the people to repentance.
  • Exile: The fall of Israel to Assyria and Judah to Babylon.

1 Chronicles

  • Genealogies: The genealogies of Israel, starting with Adam, through to the tribes and kings, including David’s line.
  • David's Reign: Focus on David’s reign, his preparation for the temple, and his relationship with God.

2 Chronicles

  • Solomon’s Reign: Solomon’s reign continues, with emphasis on the building of the temple.
  • Kingdom of Judah: Chronicles the kings of Judah, their reforms, failures, and the eventual fall to Babylon.
  • Restoration and Reforms: Reforms of kings like Hezekiah and Josiah, who seek to restore true worship of God.

Ezra

  • Return from Exile: The return of the first group of exiles from Babylon and the rebuilding of the Temple in Jerusalem.
  • Rebuilding the Temple: Ezra leads the people in the completion of the Temple, restoring the worship of God.
  • Reforms: Ezra's efforts to teach the people the law and bring them to repentance.

Nehemiah

  • Rebuilding the Walls: Nehemiah leads the efforts to rebuild the walls of Jerusalem, despite opposition.
  • Spiritual Renewal: Nehemiah and Ezra call for the people to renew their commitment to God's laws.
  • Social and Religious Reforms: Nehemiah works to restore justice, Sabbath observance, and proper worship practices.

Esther

  • Queen Esther: Esther, a Jewish woman, becomes queen of Persia and saves her people from destruction.
  • God’s Providence: God's hidden hand works through Esther and Mordecai to protect the Jews from Haman’s plot.
  • Purim Festival: The institution of the Feast of Purim to celebrate the deliverance of the Jews.

Job

  • The Suffering of Job: Job experiences extreme suffering, losing his wealth, health, and family.
  • The Friends’ Debate: Job’s friends debate with him about the reason for his suffering, offering various theological explanations.
  • God’s Response: God responds to Job, challenging him to trust in His wisdom and sovereignty.
  • Restoration: Job is restored, and his fortunes are returned, along with greater understanding of God’s ways.

Psalms

  • Praise and Worship: A collection of hymns, poems, and prayers expressing praise, lament, and thanksgiving to God.
  • The Messiah: Many psalms prophetically point to the coming Messiah, especially in David’s psalms.
  • God’s Sovereignty: Psalms affirm God’s rule over creation and His faithfulness to His people.

Proverbs

  • Wisdom: A collection of sayings emphasizing the importance of wisdom, knowledge, and understanding in daily life.
  • Fear of the Lord: The beginning of wisdom is the fear of the Lord, and a theme of living a righteous and disciplined life.
  • Practical Instruction: Guidance for relationships, work, speech, and moral behavior.

Ecclesiastes

  • The Vanity of Life: The author (traditionally Solomon) reflects on the futility of human pursuits apart from God.
  • Seeking Meaning: The search for meaning in life through wealth, pleasure, and wisdom, ultimately pointing to God’s sovereignty.
  • Fear God: The conclusion that the true purpose in life is to fear God and keep His commandments.

Song of Solomon

  • Love and Marriage: A poetic dialogue celebrating love, romance, and the beauty of marriage.
  • Symbolic Interpretations: Interpreted as an allegory of God’s love for Israel or Christ’s love for the church.

Isaiah

  • Prophecies of Judgment and Salvation: Isaiah prophecies both the judgment of Israel and the promise of salvation through the coming Messiah.
  • The Suffering Servant: Prophecies about the suffering servant, a figure representing Jesus Christ’s sacrificial work.
  • The Kingdom of God: Isaiah speaks about God’s future kingdom, peace, and restoration for Israel and the nations.

Jeremiah

  • Prophecies of Judgment: Jeremiah warns Judah of impending judgment for their sin and idolatry.
  • New Covenant: Prophecies about a new covenant God will establish with His people, writing His law on their hearts.
  • Jeremiah’s Lamentations: Jeremiah expresses deep sorrow for the fall of Jerusalem and the suffering of his people.

Lamentations

  • Destruction of Jerusalem: A collection of laments mourning the destruction of Jerusalem and the suffering of its people.
  • God's Faithfulness: Amidst the sorrow, there are expressions of hope in God's faithfulness and mercy.

Ezekiel

  • Vision of God’s Glory: Ezekiel has vivid visions of God’s glory and the impending judgment on Israel.
  • Exile and Restoration: Prophecies concerning the exile of Israel to Babylon and the future restoration of the nation.
  • Valley of Dry Bones: A famous vision illustrating God’s power to restore life and hope to His people.

Daniel

  • Faithfulness in Exile: Daniel’s unwavering faith in God despite the challenges of exile in Babylon.
  • Prophecies of Future Kingdoms: Daniel’s visions about the future kingdoms of the world and the eternal kingdom of God.
  • Fiery Furnace and Lions’ Den: Famous stories of Shadrach, Meshach, Abednego in the furnace and Daniel in the lions’ den.

Hosea

  • Hosea’s Marriage: Hosea’s marriage to an unfaithful wife symbolizes God’s relationship with unfaithful Israel.
  • God’s Love and Judgment: Despite Israel’s unfaithfulness, God offers love and promises restoration.
  • Repentance and Restoration: Hosea calls Israel to repentance and points to a future restoration of the nation.

Joel

  • The Day of the Lord: Joel speaks of the coming "Day of the Lord," a time of judgment and blessing.
  • Call to Repentance: A call to repentance for the people to return to God with fasting and prayer.
  • Promise of the Spirit: Prophecy of the outpouring of God’s Spirit, which is later fulfilled at Pentecost in Acts 2.

Amos

  • God’s Judgment on Israel: Amos warns of judgment against Israel for social injustices, idolatry, and failure to follow God’s ways.
  • Call for Justice and Righteousness: The prophet calls for justice, righteousness, and proper worship of God.
  • Hope for Restoration: Despite the judgment, God promises to restore Israel in the future.

Obadiah

  • Judgment on Edom: A prophecy of judgment against Edom for their pride and for aiding in the destruction of Jerusalem.
  • Israel’s Restoration: Despite Edom’s judgment, God promises the eventual restoration of Israel.

Jonah

  • Jonah’s Mission: Jonah is sent to the city of Nineveh to call them to repentance.
  • Reluctance and God’s Mercy: Jonah’s reluctance to obey God and the display of God’s mercy toward Nineveh.
  • God’s Compassion: Jonah's message shows that God desires the repentance of all nations, not just Israel.

Micah

  • Judgment on Israel and Judah: Micah prophesies judgment on the corrupt leaders and the people’s idolatry.
  • The Promise of a Ruler: Prophecy of the coming Messiah, born in Bethlehem, who will bring peace and justice.
  • Justice and Mercy: God calls His people to act justly, love mercy, and walk humbly with God.

Nahum

  • Judgment on Nineveh: A prophecy of the destruction of Nineveh, the capital of Assyria, for its cruelty and idolatry.
  • God’s Justice: Nahum proclaims God’s righteous judgment and His sovereignty over the nations.

Habakkuk

  • Questioning God: Habakkuk questions why God allows injustice and violence to prevail.
  • God’s Answer: God’s response is that He will use Babylon to judge Israel, but He promises that the righteous will live by faith.
  • Hope and Trust in God: Habakkuk concludes by affirming his trust in God’s sovereignty and faithfulness.

Zephaniah

  • The Day of the Lord: Zephaniah warns of the coming Day of the Lord, a day of judgment and wrath against sin.
  • Call to Repentance: A call to seek the Lord and repent before the impending judgment falls.
  • Restoration of Israel: A promise of Israel’s eventual restoration and blessings in the future.

Haggai

  • Rebuilding the Temple: Haggai encourages the people to rebuild the Temple after returning from exile in Babylon.
  • Encouragement to Work: The people are encouraged to continue working on the Temple and to prioritize worshiping God.

Zechariah

  • Visions and Prophecies: Zechariah receives visions and prophecies about the restoration of Jerusalem and the coming Messiah.
  • The Coming Messiah: Zechariah points to the coming Messiah, who will be a humble ruler and the one to bring salvation.
  • God’s Promise of Restoration: Prophecies of the future glory of Jerusalem and the spiritual renewal of Israel.

Malachi

  • Reproof of Israel: Malachi rebukes the people for their faithlessness, corrupt worship, and social injustices.
  • The Coming of Elijah: Malachi prophesies the coming of Elijah before the Day of the Lord.
  • The Promise of the Messiah: Malachi closes with a promise of a future Messiah who will bring salvation and judgment.

New Testement

Matthew

  • Birth and Early Life of Jesus: Matthew presents the genealogy and birth of Jesus, fulfilling Old Testament prophecies.
  • Teachings of Jesus: The Sermon on the Mount and many other teachings, including the parables of the kingdom of God.
  • Miracles and Ministry: Jesus performs miracles and heals the sick, demonstrating His divine authority.
  • Death and Resurrection: The crucifixion, resurrection, and the Great Commission given to His disciples.

Mark

  • Ministry of Jesus: Mark focuses on the actions of Jesus—His miracles, healings, and teachings.
  • Jesus as the Suffering Servant: Mark emphasizes Jesus’ suffering, passion, and death for the salvation of humanity.
  • Resurrection: The resurrection of Jesus and His command to go and make disciples of all nations.

Luke

  • Birth and Early Life of Jesus: Luke provides detailed accounts of the birth of John the Baptist and Jesus.
  • Ministry of Jesus: Luke highlights the compassion and mercy of Jesus, His parables, and healing miracles.
  • Death and Resurrection: The Passion narrative and the glorious resurrection, along with appearances to His disciples.

John

  • The Divinity of Jesus: John emphasizes that Jesus is the Word (Logos) made flesh, fully God and fully man.
  • Miracles and Teachings: The signs and teachings of Jesus reveal His divine nature and the path to eternal life.
  • Death and Resurrection: The crucifixion of Jesus and His ultimate victory through His resurrection.

Acts

  • Early Church Growth: Acts records the spread of the gospel and the establishment of the early church after Jesus’ ascension.
  • The Holy Spirit: The coming of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost and His role in empowering believers for ministry.
  • Paul’s Ministry: The missionary journeys of Paul and the spread of Christianity throughout the Roman Empire.

Romans

  • Righteousness through Faith: Paul explains that righteousness is obtained through faith in Jesus Christ, not through the law.
  • Justification and Salvation: The doctrine of justification by faith, and how God’s grace leads to salvation.
  • Living by the Spirit: Paul urges believers to live by the Spirit, offering their bodies as living sacrifices, and to be transformed by the renewal of their minds.

1 Corinthians

  • Divisions in the Church: Paul addresses divisions among believers, urging unity in Christ.
  • Spiritual Gifts: The use of spiritual gifts in the church, emphasizing love as the greatest gift.
  • The Resurrection: Paul defends the reality of the resurrection and its importance for Christian faith.

2 Corinthians

  • Comfort in Trials: Paul speaks about the comfort Christians receive from God in times of suffering, and the power of God in weakness.
  • The Ministry of Reconciliation: Paul discusses the ministry of reconciliation, urging believers to be ambassadors for Christ.
  • Generosity: Paul encourages the Corinthians to be generous in their giving, particularly to support the church in Jerusalem.

Galatians

  • Justification by Faith: Paul reaffirms the doctrine of justification by faith, not by the works of the law.
  • Freedom in Christ: Believers are called to live in the freedom provided by Christ, not under the yoke of the law.
  • The Fruit of the Spirit: Paul lists the fruit of the Spirit and emphasizes living a life led by the Holy Spirit.

Ephesians

  • Unity in the Body of Christ: Paul emphasizes the unity of believers, urging them to live worthy of their calling in Christ.
  • The Mystery of the Gospel: The mystery revealed in Christ is the inclusion of Gentiles into God’s people.
  • Spiritual Armor: Paul exhorts believers to put on the full armor of God to stand firm against the schemes of the enemy.

Philippians

  • Joy in Christ: Paul encourages believers to rejoice in the Lord, even in the midst of trials and persecution.
  • Humility of Christ: The example of Christ’s humility and obedience is presented as the model for Christian living.
  • Pressing Toward the Goal: Paul exhorts believers to press on toward the prize of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus.

Colossians

  • The Supremacy of Christ: Paul declares Christ’s preeminence in creation and redemption, emphasizing His fullness in all things.
  • Living in Christ: Believers are urged to set their minds on things above and to live out the reality of their new life in Christ.
  • Christian Household: Practical instructions for Christian households, including marriage and relationships between parents and children.

1 Thessalonians

  • Encouragement in Faith: Paul encourages the Thessalonian believers to remain faithful and steadfast in the face of persecution.
  • The Return of Christ: Paul speaks of the hope of Christ’s return, the resurrection of the dead, and the rapture of the church.

2 Thessalonians

  • Correction on the Day of the Lord: Paul clarifies misunderstandings about the Day of the Lord, explaining that it will not come until certain events unfold.
  • Perseverance in Trials: Paul encourages the Thessalonians to stand firm and not be deceived during difficult times.

1 Timothy

  • Instructions for Church Leadership: Paul gives Timothy instructions on appointing elders and deacons in the church.
  • Godliness in the Church: Paul emphasizes the importance of godliness, sound doctrine, and proper conduct in the church.
  • Contentment: Paul teaches the value of contentment and warns against the love of money.

2 Timothy

  • Endurance in Ministry: Paul encourages Timothy to endure hardship as a good soldier of Christ and to continue preaching the Word.
  • Paul’s Final Words: Paul reflects on his life and ministry, urging Timothy to remain faithful and continue in the faith.

Titus

  • Church Leadership: Paul gives instructions to Titus on appointing leaders and teaching sound doctrine in the church.
  • Living Godly Lives: Paul encourages believers to live lives that reflect the grace of God and to maintain good works.

Philemon

  • Reconciliation of Onesimus: Paul appeals to Philemon to forgive and receive back his runaway slave, Onesimus, as a brother in Christ.
  • Christian Forgiveness: Paul teaches about Christian forgiveness and reconciliation within the body of Christ.

Hebrews

  • The Supremacy of Christ: The author presents Christ as superior to angels, Moses, and the Levitical priesthood.
  • Faith and Perseverance: A call to persevere in the faith, looking to Jesus as the author and finisher of our faith.
  • The New Covenant: The superiority of the New Covenant in Christ, which replaces the Old Covenant and brings full forgiveness of sins.

James

  • Faith and Works: James teaches that genuine faith is evidenced by works, urging believers to live out their faith in practical ways.
  • Wisdom and Speech: James emphasizes the importance of godly wisdom, controlling the tongue, and living in humility.

1 Peter

  • Suffering for Christ: Peter encourages believers to endure suffering for Christ, as it strengthens their faith.
  • Living Holy Lives: Peter urges Christians to live holy lives in the face of trials and to be faithful witnesses of Christ.

2 Peter

  • The Day of the Lord: Peter teaches about the coming Day of the Lord and the need for holiness and godliness in anticipation of that day.
  • Warning Against False Teachers: Peter warns against false teachers who lead others astray with heresies.

1 John

  • Love and Fellowship: John emphasizes the importance of love for God and one another as signs of true fellowship with Him.
  • Jesus Christ’s Incarnation: The reality of Jesus Christ coming in the flesh and the need to abide in Him.
  • Overcoming the World: Believers are called to overcome the world and its temptations through faith in Jesus Christ.

2 John

  • Walking in Truth and Love: John exhorts believers to walk in truth and love, avoiding deceivers who do not confess Christ.

3 John

  • Hospitality and Support: John commends Gaius for his hospitality and support of faithful workers in the church.
  • Warning Against Diotrephes: John warns against Diotrephes, who refuses to welcome believers and disrupts the church.

Jude

  • Contend for the Faith: Jude urges believers to contend for the faith against false teachers who have crept into the church.
  • Warning Against Apostates: Jude warns against those who distort the grace of God into a license for immorality.

Revelation

  • Vision of Christ: John sees a vision of the glorified Christ, who holds the seven stars and walks among the seven lampstands (churches).
  • The Seven Seals, Trumpets, and Bowls: John describes the judgments that will come upon the earth, including the opening of the seven seals, the sounding of the trumpets, and the pouring out of the bowls of wrath.
  • The Final Victory: The ultimate defeat of Satan, the establishment of God’s eternal kingdom, and the new heaven and new earth.
